About

Hello! Welcome to my Webpage. I'm Rahul Jangid, a passionate researcher specializing in Continuum Mechanics, Constitutive Modeling, Non-Linear Finite Element Analysis, and Bio-mechanics.

Currently pursuing my Ph.D. in Aerospace Structure at the prestigious Indian Institute of Technology, Bombay, I hold a strong academic background with a perfect cumulative grade point average (CPI) of 10/10. I also completed my Master of Technology in Machine Design from the Indian Institute of Technology (ISM), Dhanbad, where I achieved a CPI of 9.06/10. Additionally, I obtained a Bachelor of Technology in Mechanical Engineering from the Government Engineering College, Ajmer.

Throughout my academic journey, I have received numerous scholastic achievements, including scoring a perfect 10/10 CPI in my Ph.D. coursework and being ranked 1st among the M.Tech in Machine Design batch based on academic merit. I also excelled in the GATE-Mechanical Engineering exam, scoring 704/1000.

My Ph.D. thesis, under the guidance of Prof. Krishnendu Haldar, involves the constitutive modeling and finite element analysis of brain tissue for traumatic brain injury. I have developed finite deformation-based viscoelastic constitutive models, and implemented them in Abaqus using UMAT and VUMAT subroutines. I have also developed finite element codes for hyperelastic and magneto-viscoelastic materials and implemented Gaussian process regression in finite element analysis.

I am a lifelong learner and have pursued various certifications to enhance my skills, including courses on advanced material systems, JavaScript, CSS3, HTML5, deep learning, and using Python to access web data. I am proficient in programming languages such as Fortran, Python, MATLAB, LATEX, C++, and C. I am also well-versed in software tools like Abaqus, FEAP, SOLIDWORKS, ANSYS, LS-Dyna, Hypermesh, COMSOL Multiphysics, AutoCAD, and ParaView.

In addition to my academic pursuits, I have actively taken on positions of responsibility, including serving as a Teaching Assistant for various courses related to tensors for engineers and solid mechanics. I have also held leadership roles such as Department Coordinator and Academic Unit Representative.

I am enthusiastic about collaborating with fellow professionals, researchers, and industry experts to contribute to the field of continuum mechanics and make a positive impact. If you would like to connect, discuss potential collaborations, or have any questions, please get in touch with me. Let's connect and explore the exciting possibilities together!

Research Thesis

Ph.D. Thesis

Constitutive Modeling and Finite Element Analysis Of Brain Tissue For Traumatic Brain Injury

  • Surveyed literature about constitutive modeling, mechanical characterization of brain tissue, finite element analysis, and human head finite element model
  • Development of finite deformation based viscoelastic constitutive model of white matter by using published mechanical experimental data.
  • Development of Abaqus UMAT for Ogden, Mooney, Neo-Hookean, and Blatz-Ko model.
  • Development of Abaqus VUMAT for Ogden, Mooney, Neo-Hookean, and Blatz-Ko model.
  • Non-linear FEM-based formulation, solving evolution equation for viscous deformation by using Backward Euler method, and Abaqus UMAT for the viscoelastic constitutive model.
  • Finite element Implementation of viscoelastic constitutive model in FEniCS-Python.
  • Anisotropic viscoelastic constitutive model for white matter.
  • Abaqus UMAT for Anisotropic viscoelastic constitutive model of white matter.

M.tech Thesis

To Investigate Ductile Flow Response Prediction Criteria Based on Continuum Damage Mechanics

  • Surveyed literature about continuum damage mechanics, and ductile damage criteria.
  • Performed loading-unloading test of low carbon AISI 1020 steel UTM.
  • Calculation of damage with respect to plastic strain, and critical damage value.
  • Comparison of experimental data with six different ductile damage criteria.

Research Project

Finite Element Code Development for Hyperelastic Materials

  • Stress and stiffness tensor calculation from Ogden, Mooney, and Neo-Hookean energy functions
  • Development of 2D and 3D finite element code in Matlab

User Element for Largely Deformable Magneto Viscoelastic Materials

  • Finite element formulation for magneto viscoelastic materials
  • Stress and stiffness tensor calculation from magneto viscoelastic material energy function
  • Solving evolution equations for inelastic deformation by using Backward Euler method
  • Development of 2D and 3D user element routine code by using Fortran for Abaqus and FEAP

Finite Element Analysis Using Gaussian Processes

  • Finite element formulation with Gaussian process regression
  • Apply Gaussian process regression on generated tensile and shear test data by using Python GPy
  • Use derivative of Gaussian process regression for stiffness matrix calculations
  • Development of 1D truss, 1D elasticity , and 2D elasticity finite element code in Python by using Numpy, Scipy, GPy, Matplotlib libraries
